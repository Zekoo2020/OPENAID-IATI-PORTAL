Via this transparency portal you'll find every official development aid project supported by the Dutch government and reported as open data to the IATI registry.

What data can I find on openaidNL?

This platform extracts the raw IATI data published by the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs from the IATI registry, and presents it in an easy-to-use web interface. It does not alter or interpret the data in any way.

At this transparency portal you can find every development cooperation activity supported by the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs since 2010. The portal enables you to find the activities in specific countries or regions, in sectors or by receiver organisation.

The data are reported as open data to the IATI registry. The data is quarterly updated by the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs and this portal is daily updated to reflect the data as published on the IATI registry.

Why is open data important?

Transparency of development aid enhances the effectiveness and quality of development aid. Governments of developing countries are able to plan better when they have access to accurate and up-to-date information about aid flows into their countries. Citizens in developing countries can hold their governments accountable for the spending of aid. The Dutch public gets a better picture of how public money is spent.

The value of open data is greatly increased if it can be compared and added up internationally. The International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI) aims to do just that â€“ to develop an international standard for the publication of aid data.

The team behind openaid

Openaid was designed and developed by Zimmerman & Zimmerman; they are a Dutch open-data visualisation company based in Amsterdam. Together with the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs they work together in a PPP (Public Private Partnership) project on further developing and improving the Dutch IATI transparency portal openaid.

Zimmerman & Zimmerman has become a well-known Dutch IATI specialist; they work together with partners like UNESCO, UN-Habitat, The Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO.nl), The Department for International Development (DFID) in further developing their transparency portals.

All these transparency portals run on an Open IATI Parser and API called OIPA, which was developed by Zimmerman & Zimmerman.

Openaid history

In September 2011 the Dutch Ministry of Foreign Affairs started publishing IATI data on all the development aid projects it supports. The data is published to the international IATI standard. On openaid.nl this IATI data is made accessible through an easy to use web-interface. Akvo.org initiated Openaid.nl back in 2011 and commissioned Zimmerman & Zimmerman to design, develop and maintain the OIPA codebase.
